            <h1>Node Architecture</h1><br>
            <p>Sensor nodes are tiny devices that have to support a variety of operations. Therefore they need to be design with care to optimise both their size and performances. The components of a conventianl sensor node are the following ones:
                <div class="row-fluid">
                    <div class="span6"><h3>Microcontroller</h3> The controller comprises a general-purpose processor. Due to the wireless nature of the system, this processor must be adapted to embedded applications and consume very little energy. One of the most commonly used processor in Wireless Sensor Networks is the Atmel ATMega, indeed, by balancing power-consumption and processing-speed, it presents all the qualities required. </div>

                    <div class="span6"><h3>Transceiver</h3> that can operate through different states: transmission, reception, idle mode, sleeping mode.</div>
                </div>
                <div class="row-fluid">
                    <div class="span6"><h3>Power Source</h3> that has to provide as much energy as possible at the smallest volume, lightest weight, longest lifetime. </div>

                    <div class="span6"><h3>Memory</h3> that is present under two shapes (internal one (micro-controller, volatile) and external one (flash, not volatile)).</div>
                </div>
                <div class="row-fluid">
                    <div class="span6"><h3>Sensors</h3> that monitor the environnement and collect data about it.</div>
                </div>
               </p>
